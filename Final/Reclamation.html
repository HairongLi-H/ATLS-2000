Landscape Strategy: Biodiversification
❮ ❯

Summer 2021

Location: Golden, CO

Reclamation is a comprehensive, multidisciplinary approach to solving the issue of how to reincorporate the Coors facility into the City of Golden. Through the lenses of planning, urban design, landscape architecture, and architecture, Reclamation seeks out and recognizes the needs of the community, the importance of environmental protection, and the preservation of Golden's history. Within planning, transportation is the main focus, specifically the redirection and re-imagination of multi-use paths as well as the utilization of existing railway infrastructure for transit projects. Urban design analyzes the human history of the area by transforming the main Coors facility into an energy-generating public industrial park. Through the landscape architecture lens, projects seek to return the water and surrounding land of Clear Creek to native plants and animals, fostering a more ecologically diverse and healthy site for all forms of life. The architectural scope opens other Coors buildings up for educational resources as well as affordable housing, creating an environment that fosters knowledge, growth, and compassion.
